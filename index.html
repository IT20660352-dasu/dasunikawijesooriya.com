Avoid These Erros In UI Design

To err is human, and errors happen when people engage with user interfaces. According to Don Norman, there are two categories of user errors: slips and mistakes. Slips occur when a user is on autopilot, and takes the wrong actions in service of a reasonable goal. We discuss slips and slip prevention in detail in the first article in this series. In this article we focus on mistakes. Mistakes occur when a user has developed a mental model of the interface that isn’t correct, and forms a goal that doesn’t suit the situation well. For example, many online children’s games start with a short video tutorial or with a video advertisement for another game; in our user testing with children, we noticed that, when the video looks too much like a real game, kids are tempted to interact with it, thinking that they can already start playing. In this situation, the users form and execute an inappropriate goal, largely because they interpret incorrectly what they see on the website (namely, they think that the video is the real game). Good design should help prevent such mismatches between the user’s expectations and the interface.
